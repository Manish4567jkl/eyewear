Craft

Material as argument

Hinge detail — grade 5 titanium
Metal

Aerospace-grade titanium

Every frame begins as a solid titanium billet, milled and hand-polished over eleven stages. The alloy is chosen for what it refuses to do: it will not corrode, will not fatigue at the hinge, and will not add weight the face has to carry.

Lens edge — hand-ground optical glass
Lens

Hand-ground optical glass

We use mineral glass over polymer for its optical clarity and resistance to scratching, ground and edge-polished by hand to sit flush within the frame without a visible seam.

Temple — spring-loaded barrel hinge
Construction

Assembled by hand, in small runs

Each temple is fitted with a spring-loaded barrel hinge and set by a single craftsperson from start to finish. We build in runs of under two hundred, which is slower, and, we think, worth it.
